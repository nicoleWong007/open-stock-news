# Howard Marks — Maxims and Key Quotes

Timeless wisdom from Howard Marks' memos and books.

1. "The second-level thinker takes many things into account: what is the consensus, what are the ways the consensus can be wrong, and what are the consequences of being wrong."

2. "The riskiest thing in the investment world is the belief that there is no risk."

3. "The greatest investment gains come not from being right about the future, but from being right about something that the consensus is wrong about."

4. "Investing is a matter of preparing for the future, not predicting it."

5. "You can't predict. You can prepare."

6. "The best investors are those who know what they don't know."

7. "Rule No. 1: Most things will prove to be cyclical. Rule No. 2: Some of the greatest opportunities for gain and loss come when other people forget Rule No. 1."

8. "The pendulum swings between euphoria and despair, between greed and fear, between risk tolerance and risk aversion."

9. "Being too far ahead of your time is indistinguishable from being wrong."

10. "The value of an asset is not what it's worth, but what someone will pay for it — and that depends on psychology as much as fundamentals."

11. "Superior investors are bold when others are afraid, and afraid when others are bold."

12. "The biggest profits come not from buying good things, but from buying things well."

13. "The less prudence with which others conduct their affairs, the greater the prudence with which you should conduct your own."

14. "There's a big difference between probability and outcome. Probable things fail to happen, and improbable things happen all the time."

15. "The worst loans are made at the best of times."

16. "The biggest losses come not from buying poor-quality assets, but from buying good-quality assets at the wrong price."

17. "When capital is plentiful, returns are low. When capital is scarce, returns are high."

18. "Experience is what you got when you didn't get what you wanted."

19. "It's not a question of whether the cycle will turn, but when and by how much."

20. "Successful investing requires more than just buying good things. It requires buying things well."

21. "Risk means more things can happen than will happen."

22. "Never forget the six-foot-tall man who drowned in the river that was five feet deep on average."

23. "Credit cycles are among the most powerful forces in the financial world. They can make you rich or poor."

24. "When everyone believes something is riskless, that's when it becomes most risky."

25. "Oaktree's philosophy in a nutshell: if we avoid the losers, the winners will take care of themselves."
