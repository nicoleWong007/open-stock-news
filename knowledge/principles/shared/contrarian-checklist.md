# Contrarian Investing Checklist

## Signs of Market Euphoria (Sell Signals)

When 5+ of these are present, the market is in a euphoric state. Consider reducing risk.

- [ ] **Valuation extremes**: Median stock PE above historical 90th percentile
- [ ] **Credit availability**: Banks lending freely to marginal borrowers, covenant-lite dominant
- [ ] **IPO frenzy**: Record number of IPOs, first-day pops exceeding 50%, money-losing companies going public
- [ ] **Leverage expansion**: Margin debt at or near records, household debt rising fast
- [ ] **Narrative dominance**: "This time is different" arguments widely accepted
- [ ] **Retail surge**: Massive retail participation, day trading popular, social media stock tips
- [ ] **Risk blindness**: VIX at very low levels, credit spreads near tights, volatility selling strategies popular
- [ ] **Fund flows**: Massive inflows to equity funds, outflows from defensive assets
- [ ] **Media tone**: Financial media uniformly bullish, bearish voices mocked or ignored
- [ ] **Management behavior**: Excessive M&A at high multiples, insider selling, aggressive share buybacks at peak prices

## Signs of Market Despair (Buy Signals)

When 5+ of these are present, the market is in a despair state. Consider increasing risk.

- [ ] **Valuation bargains**: Median stock PE below historical 10th percentile
- [ ] **Credit scarcity**: Banks not lending even to creditworthy borrowers, credit spreads very wide
- [ ] **IPO freeze**: No new IPOs for months, withdrawn offerings
- [ ] **Forced selling**: Margin calls, fund liquidations, redemptions forcing sales regardless of value
- [ ] **Narrative collapse**: "Stocks are dead" arguments widely accepted
- [ ] **Retail exodus**: Retail investors abandoning markets, brokerage account closures
- [ ] **Risk obsession**: VIX at very high levels, everyone talking about risk, cash hoarding
- [ ] **Fund flows**: Massive outflows from equity funds, inflows to bonds and cash
- [ ] **Media tone**: Financial media uniformly bearish, bullish voices mocked or ignored
- [ ] **Management behavior**: Share buybacks suspended, insider buying, defensive M&A at low multiples

## Pre-Contrarian Decision Questions

Before going against the consensus, answer ALL of these:

1. **What does the consensus believe?** Write it down explicitly.
2. **Why might the consensus be wrong?** Specific, evidence-based reasons.
3. **What would it take for the consensus to be right?** Acknowledge the bull case.
4. **What is my edge?** Informational, analytical, or psychological advantage.
5. **What is the worst case if I'm wrong?** Quantify the potential loss.
6. **Can I withstand the worst case?** Financially and emotionally.
7. **Is the asymmetric payoff in my favor?** Upside vs downside ratio should exceed 3:1.
8. **Am I early or wrong?** How will I know the difference? Set a time horizon.

If you cannot answer questions 1-6 with conviction, do NOT take the contrarian position.
