# Risk Assessment Framework

## Permanent Loss Probability Assessment

Evaluate the probability of permanent capital loss through four dimensions:

### 1. Fundamental Deterioration Risk (0-25%)
- Is the company's competitive position weakening?
- Is revenue declining or stagnant over multiple quarters?
- Is the industry facing structural disruption?
- Are margins compressing beyond cyclical norms?

### 2. Valuation Risk (0-25%)
- How far above intrinsic value is the current price?
- Is the market pricing in perfection or worst-case?
- Are comparable valuations at historical extremes?
- Is the discount rate assumption aggressive?

### 3. Liquidity Risk (0-25%)
- Can the position be sold within 5 days without material price impact?
- Is trading volume declining?
- Is the stock in a thin market (small-cap, emerging market)?
- Could forced selling from other holders create cascading declines?

### 4. Leverage Risk (0-25%)
- What is the debt-to-equity ratio? (>3x = high risk)
- Can the company service debt at current earnings?
- Are there near-term debt maturities that require refinancing?
- Is there off-balance-sheet leverage?

### Scoring
- **0-20%**: Low risk of permanent loss
- **20-40%**: Moderate risk — requires margin of safety
- **40-60%**: High risk — avoid unless deep value
- **60%+**: Very high risk — pass

## Risk Perversity Detection Checklist

Score each signal. If 3+ signals fire, risk perversity is present (perceived risk ≠ actual risk).

### Bull Market Danger Signals (perceived risk low, actual risk high)
- [ ] Credit spreads near historical tights (< 3rd percentile)
- [ ] VIX below 15 for extended period
- [ ] IPO market activity at frothy levels (> 200 IPOs/quarter in US)
- [ ] Covenant-lite loans dominate new issuance
- [ ] Media declares "risk has been eliminated" or "this time is different"
- [ ] Retail investor participation at extremes
- [ ] Margin debt at record levels relative to GDP

### Bear Market Opportunity Signals (perceived risk high, actual risk low)
- [ ] Credit spreads near historical wides (> 90th percentile)
- [ ] VIX above 30 for sustained period
- [ ] IPO market frozen (near zero new issues)
- [ ] Forced selling / margin calls in headlines
- [ ] Media declares "equities are dead" or "no bottom in sight"
- [ ] Institutional cash levels at record highs
- [ ] Quality companies trading below book value

## Downside Scenario Analysis

For each investment, model three scenarios:

### Bear Case (probability-weighted)
- Revenue decline: 10-20%
- Margin compression: 3-5 percentage points
- Multiple compression: 20-30%
- Expected loss from current price: X%
- Probability: 25%

### Severe Case
- Revenue decline: 20-40%
- Margin compression: 5-10 percentage points
- Multiple compression: 40-60%
- Expected loss from current price: Y%
- Probability: 10%

### Worst Case
- Permanent impairment: business model failure or bankruptcy
- Expected loss: 80-100%
- Probability: estimate based on fundamental analysis
